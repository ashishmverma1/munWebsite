Security Council

The United Nations Security Council (UNSC) is one of the six principal organs of the United Nations and is charged with the maintenance of international peace and security. Its powers include the establishment of peacekeeping operations, the establishment of international sanctions, and the authorization of military action through Security Council resolutions; it is the only UN body with the authority to issue binding resolutions to member states.

Yes, this is the Wikipedia write up. Now this is how we will do it this year in ISM MUN ‘14. We all know the Security Council has 5 permanent and 10 non-permanent members. So that adds up to 15 countries in all. And if you were to see the non-permanent countries, you wouldn’t be that pleased. Especially after seeing the likes of Lithuania, Chad and Luxembourg. No offence meant. This time, we have a 30 member country matrix with 10 permanent members and 20 non-permanent members.

Agenda for the Security Council will be the review of sanctions (all kinds mainly economic) on non-abiding nuclear nations namely Iran and North Korea.

Each year millions die in North Korea due to malnourishment and poverty while on the same hand the belligerent, oblivious dictatorship continues on with its nuclear programme, unmindful of the hardships the citizens face on the account of economic sanctions. Iran has also been on the receiving end of many harsh sanctions which have strangled economic growth in the country. The Security Council will debate the core aspects of this issue and will pass a resolution in this regard.

Veto powers will be revealed at the time of the MUN.

Country matrix:

Permanent members - People’s Republic of China, France, Russia, UK, USA, India, Germany, Japan, Australia, Brazil.

Non-permanent members - Italy, The Netherlands, South Korea, Argentina, Cuba, Venezuela, South Africa, Nigeria, Portugal, North Korea, Belgium, Canada, Iran, Vietnam, Pakistan, Belarus, Ukraine, Turkey, Saudi Arabia, Israel.
