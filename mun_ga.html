General Assembly

The United Nations General Assembly (UNGA/GA) is one of the six principal organs of the United Nations and the only one in which all member nations have equal representation. Its powers are to oversee the budget of the United Nations, appoint the non-permanent members to the Security Council, receive reports from other parts of the United Nations and make recommendations in the form of General Assembly Resolutions.

This year, the agenda of the General Assembly will be quite unlike any other. In this era of rapid economic development and industrialization, it is actually pretty simple to just ignore the environmental impacts of all that growth. Up until the point when it starts affecting all the stakeholders involved. The Earth Summit which was held in Rio in 1992 and 2012 discussed these very points. In 1997, the Kyoto Protocol was concluded and established legally binding obligations for developed countries to reduce their greenhouse gas emissions.

The United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change is the treaty and the name of the United Nations Secretariat entrusted with stabilizing greenhouse gas emissions. This remains a sticky issue with many developing and developed countries unwilling to curb emissions at the cost of reducing fossil fuel driven economic growth.

Therefore, the United Nations General Assembly has convened a special conference with sole importance being given to the modifications in the Kyoto Protocol with a forward thinking, futuristic and most importantly, realistic outlook. The General Assembly will then pass a resolution on the same issue with member countries being signatories and abiding by the guidelines framed in that resolution.

The country matrix for the General Assembly comprises of all member nations. It will be made sure that allotted countries do have certain significance and relevance in lieu of this issue.
